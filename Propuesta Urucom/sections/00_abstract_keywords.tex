\begin{abstract}
Accumulated amplifier and reference offsets can saturate intermediate nodes in geophone front ends built around a programmable system-on-chip (PSoC). This paper presents a foreground method and proof-of-concept evaluation that sequentially recenters the programmable-gain amplifier (PGA), band-pass filter (BP), summing stage (SUM), and low-pass filter (LP) using on-chip conversion and filtering resources. On two independently assembled boards and reported PGA settings spanning $\times1$--$\times50$, oscilloscope measurements show LP sample-mean offsets at the node feeding the analog-to-digital converter (ADC) of 0.4--3.9\% of the nominal 5-V full-scale span across eight board/gain combinations, versus 2.9--45.8\% under a shared fixed-reference baseline. The larger PGA residual is consistent with its wide, non-optimized acceptance band. An unlogged stationary-bench observation placed run completion near 10~s. A multichannel surface-wave record demonstrates field operation, but is not a controlled baseline comparison.
\end{abstract}
