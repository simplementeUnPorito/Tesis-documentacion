\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

Unlike precision front ends characterized near the sensor's Johnson-noise limit \cite{Kirchhoff2017}, this method trades component precision for measurement and digital assistance. Unlike software subtraction, it physically recenters observable additive DC errors within DAC authority and range. The comparison is against one shared fixed-reference baseline, not optimized per-condition fixed codes, and evaluates the complete sequence rather than each trim; per-stage ablation remains future work.

Continuous DC-servo loops introduce a settling-versus-bandwidth tradeoff \cite{Liu2020}; the foreground controller instead holds constant DAC outputs during acquisition and avoids a continuously active feedback path. Limitations include finite DAC resolution, no noise or distortion correction, and unvalidated convergence under sustained vibration, thermal drift, or slow internal filter-state transients.

\subsection{Non-Optimized PGA Acceptance}
\label{sec:pga-note}

PGA offset is only modestly smaller than baseline. With $|K_{\mathrm{PGA}}|=1$, the non-optimized $\Delta_{\mathrm{PGA}}=18$ accepts integer-error buckets centered through $\pm288$~mV; because error is rounded first, the continuous nominal edge is approximately $\pm296$~mV. The 0.24-V level in Fig.~\ref{fig:calibration_trace} lies inside it, consistent with stop-on-band rather than instability, but does not establish an accuracy floor. In the separate Board~B $\times4$ diagnostic, all $\Delta_i=1$ forced updates and reduced three external LP residuals from 148/142/157~mV to 34.4/38.5/53.5~mV. Because all bands changed and only three pairs were collected, this result is exploratory.

The only increase visible at the reported precision, Board~A SUM at $\times50$ (0.4\%FS to 0.8\%FS), is compatible with band acceptance and does not affect the downstream LP result.

\subsection{Timing and Scope}

Visible correction in Fig.~\ref{fig:calibration_trace} spans about 7~s. An unlogged bench observation placed completion near 10~s, but runs were not systematically timed. The four timeout windows sum to 57.6~s; adding the listed settling and refinement gives approximately 60.5~s, excluding stored-code verification and software/switching overhead. No laboratory timeout was observed.

One initialization selected on Board~B at $\times50$ transferred to Board~A and the reported gains. However, two boards cannot establish population scaling and fixed ordering leaves drift as a confound. Future work requires more independent boards with randomized order, temperature and parameter-sensitivity studies, per-stage ablation, and controlled field comparisons.
