\section{Platform Design}
\label{sec:platform-design}

The platform was selected for low cost, reconfigurable analog routing, hardware-assisted signal processing, reuse of acquisition resources across operating modes, and sufficient conversion resolution for both measurement and internal diagnostics. In acquisition mode, the PSoC conditions and digitizes the geophone output; in calibration mode the input chain is unchanged, but the observation path, digital-filter coefficients, and DAC-control firmware reconfigure to close a temporary mixed-signal loop.

For the $i$th stage, its slowly varying output can be represented as
\begin{equation}
 y_i^{\mathrm{DC}} = A_i y_{i-1}^{\mathrm{DC}} + b_i + K_i u_i,
 \label{eq:stage}
\end{equation}
where $A_i$ is the stage gain, $b_i$ the amplifier and reference errors, $K_i$ the signed transfer from the trim DAC to the observed node, and $u_i$ the DAC code. Adjusting only the ADDER reference forces one output toward its target but does not constrain the remaining $y_i^{\mathrm{DC}}$ values; an earlier rail cannot be recovered this way, and offsets appearing after the ADDER stay uncompensated. This ADDER-only trim was in fact the first approach tried, across five earlier prototype boards, before the per-stage architecture presented here: results were mediocre, with uncompensated drift reaching as much as 1~V on the worst boards, because trimming a single downstream node only partially resets the accumulated error and leaves both the pre-ADDER and post-ADDER stages free to drift independently.

The proposed architecture instead chooses each $u_i$ in Eq.~\ref{eq:stage} independently so that every observed $y_i^{\mathrm{DC}}$ approaches its stage reference. In the differential geophone front end considered here, all four calibration targets correspond to zero ADC counts.
