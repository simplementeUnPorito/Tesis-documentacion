\section{Platform Design}
\label{sec:platform-design}

The PSoC conditions and digitizes the geophone output in acquisition mode. In calibration mode, its measurement path, digital-filter coefficients, and DAC-control firmware form a temporary mixed-signal loop without changing the sensor input chain.

In the cascade of Fig.~\ref{fig:analog_path}, an offset introduced at any stage propagates through every downstream gain toward the ADC input. The calibration therefore exposes and trims each stage separately rather than correcting only the final ADC value.

An analog multiplexer connects one stage output at a time to the shared 18-bit delta-sigma ADC, which serves as the error sensor; software mutual exclusion prevents simultaneous connections. A switched capacitor behind the selected multiplexer MOSFET combines with its on-resistance for limited RC anti-aliasing and isolates the amplifier from a direct capacitive load.

For the $i$th stage, its slowly varying output can be represented as
\begin{equation}
 y_i^{\mathrm{DC}} = A_i y_{i-1}^{\mathrm{DC}} + b_i + K_i u_i,
 \label{eq:stage}
\end{equation}
where $A_i$ is the stage gain, $b_i$ the amplifier and reference errors, $K_i$ the signed transfer from the trim DAC to the measured node, and $u_i$ the DAC code. Before per-stage calibration, the existing SUM reference was adjusted to recenter that node. This single-point correction left the remaining $y_i^{\mathrm{DC}}$ values uncontrolled: an earlier rail could not be recovered, offsets after SUM remained uncompensated, and the approach did not extrapolate to front ends without an explicit summing node.

The proposed architecture instead chooses each $u_i$ in Eq.~\ref{eq:stage} independently so that every measured $y_i^{\mathrm{DC}}$ approaches its stage reference. In the differential geophone front end considered here, all four calibration targets correspond to zero ADC counts.

Eq.~\ref{eq:stage} is lower-triangular in stage index: $y_i^{\mathrm{DC}}$ depends on $y_{i-1}^{\mathrm{DC}}$ but not on downstream stages. Processing PGA, BP, SUM, and LP in signal-flow order therefore places $y_{i-1}^{\mathrm{DC}}$ at its target before stage $i$ is trimmed, making a single forward pass sufficient (Section~\ref{sec:self-calibration}).
