\section{Proof-of-Concept Evaluation}
\label{sec:experimental-validation}

\subsection{Prototype and Comparison Conditions}

Two independently assembled SM-24 prototypes (Boards~A and B) were compared under a \emph{shared fixed-reference baseline}, $\boldsymbol{c}_{\mathrm{base}}=(156,156,156,156)$ for (PGA, BP, SUM, LP), selected on Board~B at $\times50$ and reused at every gain, and \emph{Calibrated}, four-stage calibration followed by frozen-code acquisition. The measurement firmware disabled post-calibration EEPROM writes, so every stationary-geophone restart reloaded that vector. Thus, $N{=}10$ denotes paired restarts of one board/gain configuration, not independent devices. Normal verification made no corrective update on Board~B $\times1$ or $\times4$, producing the dashes in Table~\ref{tab:results}. A separate $\times4$ diagnostic forced three paired updates with all $\Delta_i=1$ and is excluded because its bands and sample count differ.

\subsection{Residual DC-Offset Measurement and Results}

Offsets are nominal 5-V full-scale fractions, not values normalized to instantaneous supply voltage. LP is the primary system outcome because it faces the acquisition ADC; PGA, BP, and SUM document sequential correction and expose near-rail intermediate operating points.

At each setting, a four-channel Tektronix TDS~2004B 8-bit, 60~MHz oscilloscope recorded $V_{\mathrm{mean}}$ at PGA, BP, SUM, and LP over a 100~ms post-settling window. All four probe commons were tied to $V_{\mathrm{ref}}=V_{\mathrm{DDA}}/2$, so channels measured $V_{\mathrm{stage}}-V_{\mathrm{ref}}$. PC-USB-supplied $V_{\mathrm{DDA}}$ was typically below 5~V. The unearthed PC and protective-earth-referenced oscilloscope shared a mains branch, so the scope intentionally earth-referenced $V_{\mathrm{ref}}$ \cite{TektronixTDS2000B}; other leakage paths were not characterized. Recording baseline before calibration preserved within-restart pairing, but the fixed order leaves slow drift as a possible confound.

Table~\ref{tab:results} reports the mean and sample standard deviation of $100|V_{\mathrm{mean}}|/5\,\mathrm{V}$ over ten paired restarts. The largest fixed-reference deviations approached a supply rail; because per-run supply and output-headroom margins were not logged, these are observed output deviations and may be lower bounds on the underlying linear offset. These descriptive statistics characterize within-configuration repeatability; no population-level inferential test or independent oscilloscope uncertainty budget was performed, and displayed digits do not imply sub-millivolt accuracy.

Controller acceptance uses a filtered internal ADC estimate; Table~\ref{tab:results} uses a later, asynchronous scope record without logged final ADC errors or flags. The scope residuals therefore do not directly verify $\Delta_i$, and bandwidth, elapsed-time, and residual BP-capacitor-state effects after upstream updates were not separated. Fig.~\ref{fig:calibration_trace}'s colored traces were produced offline by passing the scope records through an equivalent FIR; they are not logged controller states.

Across the eight reported board$\times$gain cells, the calibrated LP sample mean is 0.4--3.9\%FS versus 2.9--45.8\%FS under the shared fixed-reference baseline; individual calibrated observations span 0.1--5.2\%FS (Table~\ref{tab:results}). The LP mean was lower after calibration in every reported cell, without board- or gain-specific controller retuning.

\subsection{Field Operation Demonstration}

This field trial was an operational demonstration, not a controlled offset or surface-wave comparison. Fig.~\ref{fig:waterfall} shows a 10--50~m record; each geophone was planted and kept stationary during the preceding calibration. During informal field operation, a timeout was observed when a board was powered while its geophone was moving: the AC transient saturated the chain and violated the quasi-static assumption. The resolved first-arrival moveout demonstrates operation.
