\section{Experimental Validation}
\label{sec:experimental-validation}

\subsection{Prototype and Comparison Conditions}

Two independently assembled SM-24 conditioning prototypes (Board~A and Board~B), each with four VDAC references, were tested under two conditions: \emph{Default}, references hand-tuned once on Board~B at gain $\times50$ and reused unchanged; and \emph{Proposed}, sequential four-stage calibration followed by frozen-reference acquisition. Default is therefore a fixed baseline calibrated for one board and operating point, not an uncalibrated circuit. The reported settings span the PGA's $\times1$--$\times50$ range and test whether per-board and per-gain retuning is necessary; Table~\ref{tab:results} reports the resulting per-stage offsets.

\subsection{Design Target: Headroom, Not Zero}

The loop targets headroom rather than exact zero. Its deadband, timeout, estimator, and gains balance residual error against convergence time within the available DAC, ADC, and FIR capabilities (Section~\ref{sec:self-calibration}). The ADC-facing signal is centered on a 2.5~V reference, so its 0--5~V input span corresponds to a $\pm2.5$~V excursion about that operating point. Every measured node must remain in its linear, unsaturated range; Section~\ref{sec:pga-note} examines a case in which this criterion is met despite partial PGA convergence.

\subsection{Measured Headroom Recovery Across the Gain Range}

At each reported board/gain setting, a Tektronix TDS~2004B oscilloscope (4 channels, 60~MHz, 1~GS/s) measured the mean PGA, BP, SUM, and LP output over a 100~ms post-settling window under both conditions. Table~\ref{tab:results} gives absolute DC offset as a percentage of this 5~V span, with $N{=}10$ independent runs per board/gain/stage cell. LP is the ADC-facing node; PGA, BP, and SUM expose upstream saturation or nonlinearity hidden by a final-output measurement alone. Board~B $\times1$ and $\times4$ were omitted because the Default PGA offset was already inside the normal deadband, so the loop correctly converged without changing a DAC. Forcing action required manually narrowing the deadband and hence changing the test condition (Section~\ref{sec:pga-note}).

Across the eight reported board$\times$gain cells, the LP mean offset spans 0.4--3.9\%FS after calibration versus 2.9--45.8\%FS under Default (Table~\ref{tab:results}). Board~A's Default offset decreases with gain, whereas Board~B remains closer to zero at its reported settings; Proposed brings both boards below 4\%FS. Thus, within the tested boards and settings, calibration converts a board- and gain-dependent operating point into a bounded ADC-facing offset without manual retuning.
